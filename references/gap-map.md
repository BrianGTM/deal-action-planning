# Gap Map

Before you ask anything, locate the gap. Code where the deal is weak or unknown, then fire the one question matched to the darkest spot. You do not ask about all six. You ask about the one that is darkest.

Adapted from MEDDPICC, Gap Selling, and Command of the Message. The letters are not the point. The point is to find the single thing most likely to kill the deal and aim the conversation there.

## The six gaps

| Gap | The question it answers | It is dark when |
|---|---|---|
| **Pain and cost of inaction** | Is the problem real and quantified | The rep talks features and interest, not a cost the buyer is carrying today |
| **Economic buyer** | Do we have the person who can actually say yes | The rep only names users or influencers, never the person who controls the money |
| **Decision process** | Do we know how they actually buy and approve, including the late-stage paper | "They need to get it approved" with no map of who signs, or procurement, legal, and security entering with no plan for the review |
| **Champion** | Is someone selling for us when we are not in the room, and can they | A contact is warm and friendly but has spent nothing to back us internally |
| **Compelling event** | Is there a real reason to act now | The deal is "stuck" or "slipping" with no date or consequence forcing a decision |
| **Competition and do-nothing** | What is the real alternative, including no decision | The rep assumes we are the only option, or never names "they do nothing" as the likeliest loss |

## How to use it

1. Read the item. Silently rate each gap light or dark.
2. Pick the single darkest gap. That is where the deal is most likely to die.
3. Go to `probe-bank.md` and fire the probe matched to that gap.
4. If two gaps are equally dark, take the one earliest in the list. Pain and buyer beat process and competition, because a deal with no quantified pain and no economic buyer is not a deal yet.

The discipline is restraint. One dark gap, one probe, one forward action. Not a six-point interrogation.
