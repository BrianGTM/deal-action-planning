# Rollout Kit

When a manager installs this, they have to bring the team with them. This gives the manager the words for that first conversation and a short email to set expectations before the next call. These are produced outputs. The skill generates them from the manager's own context. Nobody maintains them as files.

## The shift, in one line

Our pipeline calls are going to drive the business forward. The CRM is where we look at the status. The call is where we decide what to do next and help each other do it.

## What changes for the rep

- Come with your deals already in your log, captured ahead of the call instead of narrated from memory.
- For each deal, know where it stands in one line and the one thing most likely to kill it.
- Bring a candidate next action for each deal you raise.
- We spend the call on the few deals that matter most and go deep, rather than marching through all of them.

## What the rep gets

- More help and less reporting. The room works your hardest deals with you.
- The plays that work on one deal get installed into your others.
- A safer job. Your position is strong when you are growing your approach to clients. It gets shaky when the numbers fall because the play never changed.

## Talking points for the live conversation

Use these whether it is a one-on-one or a note to the whole org.

- Why now: [the manager's real reason, like finance wanting inspection, the business scaling, or deals slipping].
- We are adults here. This is not a check on your CRM. If I needed a meeting to confirm your numbers were accurate, we would have a bigger problem to fix first.
- Here is what I will ask on every deal: where it really stands, the one thing most likely to kill it, and what we are going to do about it before next time.
- Here is the structure: a few action items and one deal we might walk away from. Focus beats coverage.

## The kickoff email

Send it before the next call. Keep it short. The shape:

- **Subject:** Our next pipeline call, coming in more focused
- **The shift:** one line on driving the business forward, with the CRM holding the status.
- **Come prepared with:** your deals in the log, one line on where each stands, the darkest gap on each, and a candidate action or two. This is the entry ticket to the call.
- **What I will be asking:** so there are no surprises, the three standing questions below.
- **Close:** this is less reporting and more help on your hardest deals.

## The three standing questions

On every deal, every call:

1. Where does it really stand?
2. What is the one thing most likely to kill it?
3. What are we going to do about it before next time, and who owns it?

These map straight to the gap map and the forward-action invariant. The reps learn them fast because they never change.
